Can Tress help us get cleaner water? Say’s Yes Swedish Researchears

“Cellulose-Based Water Purification: Harnessing the Power of Trees” Researchers at Chalmers University of Technology in Sweden have developed a simple and portable treatment system that utilizes cellulose-based material to purify contaminated water. The cellulose filter selectively adsorbs pollutants, including heavy metal ions and organic dyes, while sunlight catalyzes their breakdown.

This eco-friendly approach has the potential to remove up to 80% of pollutants, making it a valuable solution for areas with limited access to clean water. Additionally, the textile industry is testing this technology to combat toxic textile dye discharge.

Daily Practices for Water Conservation

To conserve water daily, adopt these simple practices:

  • Turn off taps while brushing teeth.
  • Fix leaks promptly.
  • Take shorter showers.
  • Use water-efficient fixtures.
  • Run full loads in appliances.
  • Collect rainwater for tasks.
  • Use mulch in gardening.
  • Reuse greywater when possible.

By following these tips, you can make a big difference in saving water every day.
